    Sanskrit Studies

    If you were to write in Kannada Letter on a slate in a school in America it will not make such sense to the people there as for them, it is nothing more than a geometrical pattern. But for us, the alphabet has a definite sound and a definite meaning.

    What is an Alphabet? It is the form we give to a particular sound. Sound takes the form of different alphabets in different languages. A language is composed of alphabets which signify different sounds. These alphabets when arranged in a particular fashion result in words. Hence the most basic form of sound is alphabet. Though there are many languages in this world Sanskrit has the unique disction of being called the divine language. Sanskrit is different from other languages in the sense that it has a unique richness as well as engmatic experience on those who speak the languages.

    Sound has the inherent quality of energizing from silence or vaccum. But it soon takes on the shape of alphabets. All sounds have a cosmic quality about them selves.

    The positive vibrations that the sound generates are known as the 'Aa' and 'Ka' and these are the basic of all sounds and are crucial for the development language. Mantras(sounds) take the form of yantras(form) and the technique of knowing mantra and tantra is called Tantra.

    To know more about how rhythm becomes Vedas, Vedas become mantras, mantras become yantras and how yantra assume their power, visit the centre for vedic studies, which is one of the nine such centres at Vyasa Veda Peeta.

    Centre for Ayurveda

    Ayurveda means, literally, science of life "anu" being the Sanskrit word for "life" and "veda" for knowledge, As its name suggests, Ayurveda is a body of knowledge.

    Ayurveda not only aims to eradicate the underlying causes of disease, it begins from and altogether deeper level, creating a balance within body and soul that acts as an effective defense against illness. In Ayurveda the prevention of disease is even more important than its cure.

    According to Ayurveda, however just about everything is connected to your state of health. Not only does it see you in a holistic light there are no barriers between body, mind and spirit in Ayurveda it sees you as a part of the environment, nature and the universe beyond. For you to be healthy and content, you must be in harmony not just within yourself but with the worlds around you.

    Harmony and balance these are the starting points of Ayurveda and the place to which it always returns. Ayurveda turns this sense of harmony into a goal that you can move towards step by step, as you develop an understanding and experience of how the mind and body works and of some basic laws of nature.

    Shiva Shakti Chaitanya Ganapati

    This one of a kind of Idol of Ganapathi has the face of Lord Shiva on the left side and Shakthi on the right forming Ganapathi in the middle was envisaged by Gurumatha Amma in meditation.

    'Lambodhara' is Manthra Sarira of Shakthi. When the union of Shiva and Shakthi happens in Samadhi, Chaithanya Ganapathi becomes root cause for the awakening of Kundalini.

    The right hemisphere of our brain is 'Shakthi' or Emotional brain, and the left hemisphere is 'Shiva' the Logical Brain.

    The union of logic and emotion, Shiva and Shakthi awakens the 'Chaithanya' which is Shiva Shakthi Chaithanya Ganapathi.

    Bhudevi Karunakshi

    Poojya Gurumatha Amma saw in her meditation that a temple has to be built in the form of 'Bupura', the first enclave in Srichakra, at the foot of the hillock. Thus Bupura is the abode of Goddess Karunakshi.

    Lotus feet of Bhudevi Karunakshi is the idol installed which represents the earth principle (Prithvi thatva).

    The uniqueness of this temple is that firstly, Devi here is 'Nirakara' (formless) and only Her feet has been installed with 'Muladhara' chakra (the primal energy centre in the shatchakras) as the base

    The temple has been built in the form of Bupura consisting of 4 doors (dwaras). The door facing east has lord Ganapathi, the west facing dwara has "LUM" mantra (the mantra of Muladhara chakra), the south dwara has preceeding deity of Panchamukhi Gayathri, and the north dwara has the yantra of Muladhara chakra.

    This is like having darshan of four temples at a time. One gets to have this rare darshan only once in a year on Gurupoornima day.

    Karuneshwara linga

    Prior to the prathisthapana as Poojya Gurumatha Amma was observing 48 days vratha, On the 28th day when she was meditating upon the shiva mantra she had Shivalinga darshana.

    As a result of this Sri Karuneshwara temple was built within 21 days.

    Sri Karuneshwara linga which has been installed was a miracle.

    On the day Amma had linga darshana a brahmin pandit from Soth Canara had intuition and brought here this Saligrama found in the river Narmada. But this is history. Amma called this as "Jyothirlinga". Now Sri Karuneshwara has been installed in front of Sri Karunakshi's Lotus feet.

    Rudrabhisheka, pradosha pooja are performed here with great devotion.

    On Mahashivarathri day devotees can have the darshan of Sri Karuneshwara and Devi's feet simultaneously from the east dwara of Sri Karuneshwara.

    This happens only on Mahashivarathri day every year.

    Please remember to notice the 'swarnarekha' on this Jyothriling when you visit the temple. The swarnarekha or the '3 granthis' formed when Poojya Gurumatha was performing Abhishekha to the Lord.

    Karma yogis offer sacrifice to the gods, Gnana yogis surrender their mental, spiritual and physical activity into the supreme fire.

    Through yogic practices like ashtanga yoga, particularly specific mudras, heat is generated and spreads to the crown chakra, thereby melting the frozen nectar, Which then permeates through all the nadis (channels of energy) in the body.

    This is the rahastarpana, the offering of Rahoyoga. Performing inner yagas (sacrifice) is an important and necesary process to glimpse the divinity within ourselves and in the entire world.

    As we introspect and touch the truth of our nature buried within ourseles, we become more refined, our energy increases and becomes more subtle. As a result, the desire to make the transition from mediocrity to greatness surfaces within each one of us.

    Jeevanamukthi

    Jeevanamukthi or Supportless Support is another way of contributing to a selfless cause and the cause of the Centre. The day to day activities of the Centre is possible because of the small monthly contribution made by number of people. Good deeds are acts of service which are done without any intention of benefit.

    Jeevanamukthi is the process through which a drop merges into an ocean and becomes ocean. It becomes a way of achieving liberation from the miseries of life. It is a vow one make to oneself. It is one of the meanings of support less support.

    You are most welcome to be part of this process.
